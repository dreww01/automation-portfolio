Case Study · LinkedIn Automation
LinkedIn Auto-Poster
A fully automated content pipeline that writes and publishes a LinkedIn post every morning without any human input.

100%

The Problem

Consistent LinkedIn posting is one of the highest-ROI activities for consultants and coaches — but it requires daily effort. Most professionals either post inconsistently or outsource it at high cost. The client needed to stay visible on LinkedIn without spending time on it every morning.

What I Built

A scheduled n8n workflow triggers at 9am daily. It calls the OpenAI API with a crafted prompt to generate a LinkedIn-optimised post. The content is extracted and validated — if empty or malformed, the error path fires immediately.

On success, the post is published via the LinkedIn API, and a full log entry is written to Airtable — including the post content, word count, model used, post URL, and timestamp.

On failure, a formatted error message is built and sent to a Slack webhook so the issue gets noticed and fixed before the next day's post. Failed attempts are also logged to Airtable with their error reason.

Why It Works

The dual logging path — success and failure — means the client always knows what's happening. The workflow doesn't just automate the task, it automates the monitoring too. One system handles writing, publishing, logging, and alerting.

Case Study · CRM Automation
AI Lead Qualification & Instant Follow-up
An AI agent that reads every inbound lead, scores their intent, and sends a personalised email — all before a human has opened their laptop.
<30s
form to personalised email
3-tier
Hot / Warm / Cold routing
0
duplicate leads processed
The Problem

The client — a business coach — was receiving leads from an n8n form and manually reading, qualifying, and replying to each one. Response time was slow, follow-up was inconsistent, and hot leads were being treated the same as cold ones.

What I Built

When a form is submitted, the system first checks Airtable for the email — if the lead already exists, it stops immediately to prevent duplicate processing. New leads are passed to a Gemini AI agent with a detailed scoring rubric.

The agent reads the lead's pain point, business context, and readiness to invest, then outputs a JSON object with a category (Hot/Warm/Cold), a 0–100 score, a one-sentence reason, and a fully written personalised follow-up email with the correct tone per category.

A Switch node routes by temperature. Hot leads get an email with a Cal.com booking link, a record saved to Airtable with status "Call Booked", and an instant WhatsApp alert via Evolution API. Warm and Cold leads get their own toned-down emails and Airtable records with "Engaged" status.

A custom Code node validates and normalises the AI output before it touches any downstream node — handling key mismatches, case variations, and malformed JSON so the workflow never breaks on an unexpected AI response.

Why It Works

The key insight is that not every lead deserves the same response. The AI doesn't just write emails — it makes a real business decision about how urgently to treat each person. The client now wakes up to replied leads, scored records, and WhatsApp pings for the ones that matter most.

Case Study · Email Sequences
Automated Email Drip Sequence
A time-aware follow-up system that continues the conversation with Warm and Cold leads over 3–7 days, without any manual scheduling.
3
emails for warm leads
2
emails for cold leads
Daily
trigger checks Airtable
The Problem

After the qualification workflow fires the first email, most leads don't reply immediately. Without a follow-up sequence, warm and cold leads go cold permanently. The client needed a system that would continue nurturing leads on a schedule without any manual intervention.

What I Built

This workflow is the second half of the lead system. It runs every morning at 9am and makes three parallel Airtable queries — one for Warm leads ready for Email 2 (3+ days since Email 1), one for Warm leads ready for Email 3 (4+ days since Email 2), and one for Cold leads ready for their final email (5+ days since Email 1).

Each branch sends a different email with the correct tone. Warm Email 2 is a social proof email — it shares a relatable insight about entrepreneurs who feel stuck and positions coaching as the solution. Warm Email 3 is a soft close — no pressure, just an open door. Cold Email 2 is a one-line final check-in, no selling.

After each send, the Airtable record is updated: Email Sequence advances to the next stage and the sent timestamp is recorded. This prevents the same lead from receiving an email twice and is how the daily trigger knows exactly who's ready for what.

Why It Works

The system uses Airtable as its source of truth. The filter formula checks both the sequence stage and the exact date, so timing is precise. The workflow doesn't need to store state anywhere else — the database is the state machine. Leads move through the sequence automatically and stop when done.

Case Study · Booking Automation
Cal.com Booking Automation
The final piece of the lead system. When a prospect books a call, everything is handled automatically — confirmation, logging, and instant host notification.
Real-time
webhook triggers on booking
2 tables
Airtable sync per booking
WhatsApp
host notified instantly
The Problem

When a hot lead books a discovery call, the host was manually checking Cal.com for new bookings, copying details into Airtable, and sending a confirmation email. This was slow, error-prone, and meant the host sometimes didn't know a call was booked until they opened the app.

What I Built

Cal.com fires a webhook to n8n the moment a booking is created. A Code node extracts the relevant fields from the complex webhook payload — attendee name, email, timezone, meeting URL, start/end time, and booking ID — into a clean flat object.

The attendee email is used to look up the matching lead record in Airtable. Their existing data (name, company, phone, AI score) is pulled and combined with the booking details to create a new record in the "Booked Calls" table, marked as "Upcoming".

Two actions fire in parallel: a confirmation email via Gmail is sent to the attendee with the meeting link, time, and what to expect — and a WhatsApp message via Evolution API alerts the host with the attendee's name, phone, email, and meeting time.

Why It Works

This completes the full lead lifecycle: form → qualify → nurture → book → confirm. Every step is automated. The host only needs to show up to the call. The attendee gets a professional confirmation within seconds. And Airtable always reflects the current state of every lead and booking.
